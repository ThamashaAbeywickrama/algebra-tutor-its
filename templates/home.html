What are Linear Equations?

Linear equations are algebraic equations where the highest power of the variable is 1. They form straight lines when graphed and are the foundation of algebra.


General Form: ax + b = c

Where 'a' is the coefficient, 'b' is the constant, and 'x' is the variable we solve for.


Example: 2x + 3 = 7 (Solution: x = 2)


Linear equations are used everywhere - from calculating distances and speeds to budgeting and financial planning!
